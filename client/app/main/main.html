Welcome

Thanks for stopping by the web site of Long Island's premiere oldies dance band. At it's core, "Express" is a six piece band consisting of vocals, guitar, keyboard, percussion, sax and drums. We specialize in music covering the 40's, 50's, 60's, 70's, 80's and 90's.

Express has performed with such great groups as, "The Capris", "The Passions", "The Mystics", "Demensions", "Dion and the Belmonts", "The Drifters", and "The Coasters." Click around and discover how we can accommodate your next occasion.

"Express" established it's roots back in the mid 1950's with the oldest of the Dana Brothers and the group's leader, Frank Dana ( rhythm guitar, lead vocals, and falsetto), playing in the group "Rusty and the Rock-A-Tones."

Mike Dana ( keyboards, falsetto, lead vocals and musical arranger) joined the "Rock-A-Tones" in 1957, at the age of 12 when they became the house band for St. Thomas Aquinas Church on Flatlands Avenue in Brooklyn. St. Thomas sponsored many of the 50's rock and roll shows where the "Rock-A-Tones" backed such groups as "Dion and the Belmonts", "The Passions", "The Mystics", and "The Dimensions", to name a few. In 1960 Frank Dana went into the service and the "Rock-A-Tones" disbanded.

After joining the LIRR in 1967, he met Frank Sellers and formed a group called "Frank Sellers and the Coachmen", playing mostly country music, and some 50's and 60's. The group stayed together until 1981, when Rick Frase (sax and vocal bass) and Frank left to form "NiteOwl Express", playing mostly 50's and 60's music and some country. Mike Dana rejoined his brother's group in 1983, and the youngest of the Dana Brothers, Don (Lead vocals, 2nd tenor and percussion), joined in 1985. Tony Orlando "NiteOwl Express" drummer passed away in 1990, and Frankie B. (Frank Bell) became the group's drummer. Frankie B. passed away in 2014 and Bill C. became the groups drummer. After following the band for about a year, and being featured as a guest singer, Eddie Diamond (lead doo-wop vocals) rounded out the group in 1993 at which time the groups name was shortened to "Express." In 2004, Ricky Frase (sax, vocal bass) passed away. Approximately one year later, Richie B. became the groups sax player. Unfortunately Richie B. passed away in 2012 and Jimmy K. became the group's regular sax player. On February 1st 2011, the groups founder, Frank Dana passed away. Cal Tee is now the group's regular guitarist.
